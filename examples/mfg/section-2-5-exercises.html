47.

In a lab assignment, a student reports that a chemical reaction required \(200\) minutes to complete. Let \(t\) represent the actual time of the reaction.

  1. Write an absolute value inequality for \(t\text{,}\) assuming that the student rounded his answer to the nearest \(100\) minutes. Give the smallest and largest possible value for \(t\text{.}\)

  2. Write an absolute value inequality for \(t\text{,}\) assuming that the student rounded his answer to the nearest minute. Give the smallest and largest possible value for \(t\text{.}\)

  3. Write an absolute value inequality for \(t\text{,}\) assuming that the student rounded his answer to the nearest \(0.1\) minute. Give the smallest and largest possible value for \(t\text{.}\)

Hint

What is the shortest time that would round to \(200\) minutes? The greatest time?

Answer

  1. \(\abs{t - 200}\lt 50\text{,}\) \(~150\le t\lt 250\)

  2. \(\abs{t - 200}\lt 0.5\text{,}\) \(~199.5\le t\lt 200.5\)

  3. \(\abs{t - 200}\lt 0.05\text{,}\) \(~199.95\le t\lt 200.05\)

48.

An espresso machine has a square metal plate. The side of the plate is \(2\pm 0.01\) cm.

  1. Write an absolute value inequality for the length of the side, \(x\text{.}\) Give the smallest and largest possible value for s.

  2. Compute the smallest and largest possible area of the plate, including units.

  3. Write an absolute value inequality for the area, \(A\text{.}\)

49.

  1. Write the piecewise definition for \(\abs{3x - 6}\text{.}\)

  2. Use your answer to part (a) to write two inequalities that together are equivalent to \(\abs{3x - 6}\lt 9\text{.}\)

  3. Solve the inequalities in part (b) and check that the solutions agree with the solutions of \(\abs{3x - 6}\lt 9\text{.}\)

  4. Show that \(\abs{3x - 6}\lt 9\) is equivalent to the compound inequality \(-9\lt 3x - 6\lt 9\text{.}\)

Answer

  1. \(\abs{3x-6} = \begin{cases} -(3x-6) \amp \text{if } x\lt 2 \\ 3x-6 \amp \text{if } x\ge 2 \end{cases}\)

  2. \(-(3x - 6)\le 9\text{,}\) \(~3x - 6\lt 9\)

  3. \(-1\lt x\lt 5\)

  4. The solutions are the same.

50.

  1. Write the piecewise definition for \(\abs{3x - 6}\text{.}\)

  2. Use your answer to part (a) to write two inequalities that together are equivalent to \(\abs{3x - 6}\gt 9\text{.}\)

  3. Solve the inequalities in part (b) and check that the solutions agree with the solutions of \(\abs{3x - 6}\gt 9\text{.}\)

  4. Show that \(\abs{3x - 6}\gt 9\) is equivalent to the compound inequality \(3x - 6\lt -9~\) or \(~3x-6\gt 9\text{.}\)

51.

  1. Write the piecewise definition for \(\abs{2x+5}\text{.}\)

  2. Use your answer to part (a) to write two inequalities that together are equivalent to \(\abs{2x+5}\gt 7\text{.}\)

  3. Solve the inequalities in part (b) and check that the solutions agree with the solutions of \(\abs{2x+5}\gt 7\text{.}\)

  4. Show that \(\abs{2x+5}\gt 7\) is equivalent to the compound inequality \(2x+5\lt -7~\) or \(~2x+5\gt 7\text{.}\)

Answer

  1. \(\abs{2x+5} = \begin{cases} -(2x+5) \amp \text{if } x\lt \dfrac{-5}{2} \\ 2x+5 \amp \text{if } x\ge \dfrac{-5}{2} \end{cases}\)

  2. \(-(2x+5)\gt 7\text{,}\) \(~2x+5\gt 7\)

  3. \(x\lt -6\) or \(~x\gt 1\)

  4. The solutions are the same.

52.

  1. Write the piecewise definition for \(\abs{2x+5}\text{.}\)

  2. Use your answer to part (a) to write two inequalities that together are equivalent to \(\abs{2x+5}\lt 7\text{.}\)

For Problems 53–56, graph the function and answer the questions.

53.

\(f (x) = \abs{x + 4} + \abs{x - 4}\)

  1. Using your graph, write a piecewise formula for \(f (x)\text{.}\)

  2. Experiment by graphing \(g(x) = \abs{x + p} + \abs{x - q}\) for different positive values of \(p\) and \(q\text{.}\) Make a conjecture about how the graph depends on \(p\) and \(q\text{.}\)

  3. Write a piecewise formula for \(g(x) = \abs{x + p} + \abs{x - q}\text{.}\)

Answer

  1. \(f(x) = \begin{cases} -2x, \amp x\lt -4 \\ 8, \amp -4\le x\le 4 \\ 2x, \amp x\gt 4 \end{cases} \)

  2. The graphs looks like like a trough. The middle horizontal section is \(y = p + q\) for \(-p \le x\le q\text{,}\) the left side, \(x\lt -p\text{,}\) has slope \(-2\) and the right side, \(x\gt q\text{,}\) has slope \(2\text{.}\)

  3. \(g(x) = \begin{cases} -2x+q-p, \amp x\lt -p \\ p+q, \amp -p\le x\le q \\ 2x+p-q, \amp x\gt q \end{cases} \)

54.

\(f (x) = \abs{x + 4} - \abs{x - 4}\)

  1. Using your graph, write a piecewise formula for \(f (x)\text{.}\)

  2. Experiment by graphing \(g(x) = \abs{x + p} - \abs{x - q}\) for different positive values of \(p\) and \(q\text{.}\) Make a conjecture about how the graph depends on \(p\) and \(q\text{.}\)

55.

\(f (x) = \abs{x + 4} +\abs{x} + \abs{x - 4}\)

  1. Using your graph, write a piecewise formula for \(f (x)\text{.}\)

  2. What is the minimum value of \(f (x)\text{?}\)

  3. If \(p, q \ge 0\text{,}\) what is the minimum value of \(g(x) = \abs{x + p}+\abs{x} + \abs{x - q}\text{?}\)

Answer

  1. \(f(x) = \begin{cases} -3x, \amp x\lt -4 \\ -x+8, \amp -4\le x\le 0 \\ x+8, \amp 0\lt x\lt 4 \\ 3x, \amp x\ge 4 \end{cases} \)

  2. \(8\)

  3. \(p+q\)

\(f(x) = \abs{x + 4} -\abs{x}+ \abs{x - 4}\)

  1. Using your graph, write a piecewise formula for \(f (x)\text{.}\)

  2. What is the minimum value of \(f (x)\text{?}\)

  3. If \(p, q \ge 0\text{,}\) what is the minimum value of \(g(x) = \abs{x + p}-\abs{x} + \abs{x - q}\text{?}\)

57.

A small pottery is setting up a workshop to produce mugs. Three machines are located on a long table, as shown in the figure. The potter must use each machine once in the course of producing a mug. Let \(x\) represent the coordinate of the potter's station.

  1. Write expressions for the distance from the potter's station to each of the machines.

  2. Write a function that gives the sum of the distances from the potter's station to the three machines.

  3. Graph your function for \(-20\le x\le 30\text{.}\) Where should the potter stand in order to minimize the distance she must walk to the machines?

Answer

  1. \(\abs{x + 12}\text{,}\) \(\abs{x + 4}\text{,}\) \(\abs{x - 24}\)

  2. \(f(x)=\abs{x + 12}+\abs{x + 4}+\abs{x - 24}\)

  3. At \(x\)-coordinate \(-4\)
