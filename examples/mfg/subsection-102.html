Subsection 6.2 Tables of Values

Just as for functions of a single variable, we can use tables to describe functions of two variables, \(z = f (x, y)\text{.}\) The row and column headings show the values of the two input variables, and the table entries show the values of the output variable.

Example 6.2.3.

Windchill is a function of two variables, temperature and wind speed, or \(W = f(s, t)\text{.}\) The table shows the windchill factor for various combinations of temperature and wind speed.

Windchill Factors
Temperature (\(\degree\)F)
Wind speed (mph) \(35\) \(30\) \(25\) \(20\) \(15\) \(10\) \(5\) \(0\)
\(5\) \(33\) \(27\) \(21\) \(16\) \(12\) \(7\) \(0\) \(-5\)
\(10\) \(22\) \(16\) \(10\) \(3\) \(-3\) \(-9\) \(-15\) \(-22\)
\(15\) \(16\) \(9\) \(2\) \(-5\) \(-11\) \(-18\) \(-25\) \(-31\)
\(20\) \(12\) \(4\) \(-3\) \(-10\) \(-17\) \(-24\) \(-31\) \(-39\)
\(25\) \(8\) \(1\) \(-7\) \(-15\) \(-22\) \(-29\) \(-36\) \(-44\)

  1. What is the windchill factor when the temperature is \(15\degree\)F and the wind is blowing at \(20\) mph? Write this fact with function notation.

  2. Find a value for \(t\) so that \(f (10, t) =-15\text{.}\) What does this equation tell you about the windchill factor?

  3. Solve the equation \(f (s, 30) = 1\text{.}\) What does this tell you about the windchill factor?

Solution

  1. We look in the row for \(20\) mph and the column for \(15\degree\text{.}\) The associated windchill factor is \(-17\text{,}\) so \(f(20, 15) = -17\text{.}\)

  2. We look in the row for \(s = 10\) until we find the windchill factor of \(W = -15\text{.}\) The column heading for that entry is \(5\text{,}\) so \(t = 5\text{.}\) When the wind speed is \(10\) mph and the windchill factor is \(-15\text{,}\) the temperature is \(5\degree\)F.

  3. In the \(t = 30\degree\)F column, we find the windchill factor of \(1\) in the \(25\)-mph row, so \(s = 25\text{.}\) The wind speed is \(25\) mph when the temperature is \(30\degree\)F and the windchill factor is \(1\text{.}\)

Checkpoint 6.2.4.

A retirement plan requires employees to put aside a fixed amount of money each year until retirement. The amount accumulated, \(A\text{,}\) includes \(8\)% annual interest on employee's annual contribution, \(c\text{.}\) \(A\) is a function of \(c\) and the number of years, \(t\text{,}\) that the employee makes contributions, so \(A = f(c, t)\text{.}\)

Retirement Fund Balance
Number of years of contributions
Annual contribution \(10\) \(20\) \(30\) \(40\) \(50\)
\(500\) \(7243\) \(22,881\) \(56,642\) \(129,528\) \(286,885\)
\(1000\) \(14,487\) \(45,762\) \(113,283\) \(259,057\) \(573,770\)
\(1500\) \(21,730\) \(68,643\) \(169,925\) \(388,585\) \(860,655\)
\(2000\) \(28,973\) \(91,524\) \(226,566\) \(518,113\) \(1,147,540\)
\(2500\) \(43,460\) \(137,286\) \(339,850\) \(777,170\) \(1,721,310\)
\(3000\) \(50,703\) \(160,167\) \(396,491\) \(906,698\) \(2,008,196\)

  1. How much will an employee accumulate if she contributes $500 a year for \(40\) years? Write your answer with function notation.

  2. How much must she contribute each year in order to accumulate $573,770 after \(50\) years? Write your answer with function notation.

  3. Find a value of \(t\) that solves the equation \(137,286 = f(2500, t)\text{.}\) What does this equation tell you about the retirement fund?

Answer

  1. $\(129,528, ~~f(500, 40) = 129,528\)

  2. $\(1000, ~~ f(c, 50) = 573,770\)

  3. \(20\) years. If you contribute $\(2500\) per year for \(20\) years, you will accumulate $\(137,286\text{.}\)
