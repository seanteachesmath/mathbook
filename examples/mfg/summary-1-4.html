Subsection 5.8 Section Summary

¶

Subsubsection 5.8.1 Vocabulary

Look up the definitions of new terms in the Glossary.

  • Ratio

  • Rate of change

  • Slope

  • Scale factor

Subsubsection 5.8.2 CONCEPTS

  1. We can use ratios to compare quantities.

  2. The slope ratio, \(\dfrac{\text{change in y-coordinate}}{\text{change in }x\text{-coordinate}}\text{,}\) measures the steepness of a line.

  3. Notation for slope: \(m = \dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}\text{,}\) \(\Delta x \ne 0\text{.}\)

  4. Formula for slope: \(m =\dfrac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}\text{,}\) \(~~x_2\ne x_1 \)

  5. Formula for slope: \(m =\dfrac{f(x_2) - f(x_1)}{x_2 - x_1}\text{,}\) \(~~x_2\ne x_1 \)

  6. Lines have constant slope.

  7. Slope is a scale factor that tells us how many units \(\Delta y\) increases for each unit increase in \(\Delta x\) as we move along the line.

  8. The slope gives us the rate of change.

Subsubsection 5.8.3 STUDY QUESTIONS

  1. Explain how to compare prices with unit pricing.

  2. Why is \(\Delta y\) the numerator of the slope ratio and \(\Delta x\) the denominator?

  3. Which line is steeper, one with \(m = -2\) or one with \(m = -5\text{?}\)

  4. A classmate says that you must always use the intercepts to calculate the slope of a line. Do you agree? Explain.

  5. In an application, what does the slope of the graph tell you about the situation?
