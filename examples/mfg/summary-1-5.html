Look up the definitions of new terms in the Glossary.

  • Point-slope form

  • Parameter

Subsubsection 6.5.2 CONCEPTS

  1. Linear functions form a two-parameter family, \(f (x) = b + mx\text{.}\)

  2. The initial value of a linear function and the \(y\)-intercept of its graph are given by \(b\text{.}\) The rate of change of the function and the slope of its graph are given by \(m\text{.}\)

  3. The slope-intercept form, \(y = b + mx\text{,}\) is useful when we know the initial value and the rate of change.

  4. The point-slope form, \(y = y_1 + m(x - x_1)\text{,}\) is useful when we know the rate of change and one point on the line.

Subsubsection 6.5.3 STUDY QUESTIONS

  1. How can you put a linear equation into slope-intercept form?

  2. What do the coefficients in the slope-intercept form tell you about the line?

  3. Explain how to graph a line using the slope-intercept method.

  4. Explain how to find an equation for a line from its graph.

  5. Explain how to use the point-slope form for a linear equation.

  6. Francine says that the slope of the line \(y = 4x - 6\) is \(4x\text{.}\) Is she correct? Explain your answer

  7. Delbert says that the slope of the line \(3x - 4y = 8\) is \(3\text{.}\) Is he correct? Explain your answer.
